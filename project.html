The Challenge

Making mental health
feel less clinical.

Existing mental wellness apps felt like productivity tools in disguise — streaks, badges, push notifications guilt-tripping users back. The brief was to make something that met people where they were, not where the algorithm wanted them to be.

I led research, IA, visual design, and handoff across a 12-week sprint with one engineer and a product manager.

Research & Synthesis

Twenty user interviews, competitive teardowns, and a survey of 140 Gen Z respondents. The key finding: users didn't want to be reminded they weren't doing well — they wanted to be met with curiosity, not judgment.
